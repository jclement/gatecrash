A little love letter to passkeys

No passwords here — and that's the point. A passkey is a cryptographic key that lives on your device (your laptop, phone, or a hardware key) and is unlocked with the thing you already use to unlock it: a fingerprint, your face, or a PIN. Gatecrash only ever sees the public half, so there's nothing to leak, phish, or reuse.

  • Phishing-resistant. A passkey is bound to this site's domain — it simply won't work on a look-alike.
  • Nothing to remember or type. No password to forget, rotate, or have stolen in a breach.
  • One per device, as many as you like. Add a passkey on each device you sign in from — give them names so you can tell them apart and remove one if a device is lost.

Tip: many platforms sync passkeys through your password manager or iCloud/Google account, so a passkey you create on your phone can follow you to your laptop.
